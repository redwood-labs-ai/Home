Redwood Labs

Redwood is a full‑stack AI company built on one idea: every business deserves an agent designed precisely for its hardest problem, and every great agent deserves a clear path to market. We started by fixing a small sales and marketing bottleneck for Flashpoint, and realized each subagent we built could be reused, recombined, and reassembled to solve entirely different problems across industries.

Every company deserves its own AI plan. Not a chatbot. Not a dashboard. A dynamic system tailored to its problems and priced for the value it delivers. Today that future is blocked by specialized SaaS and costly consulting that fail to scale, adapt, or solve problems at their core.

Redwood Labs is reimagining AI from first principles. Inspired by functional programming, we build subagents as small reliable functions each designed to perform a specific task. Just like a few Lego blocks can build anything from a car to a castle, different combinations of subagents unlock entirely new agentic workflows that solve complex problems in sales, healthcare, education, or logistics in hours, not quarters.

Our moat is motion. Every deployment expands the subagent library, improves pricing intelligence, broadens distribution, and sharpens our ability to solve harder problems, faster. It’s a compounding loop: agents create value, value grows the network, and the network builds smarter agents.

We are engineers, mathematicians, and problem solvers who graduated from Harvard and Cambridge, with experience at BigHat Biosciences, Dana-Farber, Beth Israel, and Accenture. We’ve published at NeurIPS, contributed original results to Hodge theory, and built AI systems that solve high-stakes problems where it matters most. We believe agentic AI is not just software. It is a catalyst for progress, a partner in creation, and a force that unlocks human potential at scale. That is the future we are building at Redwood Labs.

— Jarell, Zad, and Bryan
