Redwood Labs

Redwood is a full‑stack AI company built on the idea that every business deserves an agent designed precisely for its hardest problem, and every great agent deserves a clear path to market. We started by fixing a small sales and marketing bottleneck for Flashpoint, and realized each subagent we built could be reused, recombined, and reassembled to solve entirely different problems across industries.

Every company deserves its own AI plan. Not a chatbot. Not a dashboard. A dynamic system tailored to its problems and priced for the value it delivers. Today that future's blocked by niche SaaS products and costly consulting firms that fail to scale, adapt, or solve problems at their core.

Redwood is reimagining AI from first principles. Inspired by functional programming, we build subagents: small reliable functions designed to perform specific industry-agnostic tasks. Just as a few Lego blocks can build anything from cars to castles, the right combinations of subagents unlock entirely new agentic workflows. In turn, these workflows solve complex problems across industries — from sales to healthcare to education to logistics — in hours instead of quarters.

Our moat is motion. Every deployment expands the subagent library, improves pricing intelligence, broadens distribution, and sharpens our ability to solve harder problems, faster. Every sale strengthens a compounding loop: agents create value, value grows the network, and the network builds smarter agents.

We are engineers, mathematicians, and problem solvers who graduated from Harvard and Cambridge, with experience at BigHat Biosciences, Dana-Farber, Beth Israel, and Accenture. We have published at NeurIPS, contributed original results in Hodge theory, and built AI systems that solve high-stakes problems where it matters most. We believe agentic AI isn't just software. It's a catalyst for progress, a partner in creation, and a force that unlocks human potential at scale. That's the future we're building at Redwood.

— Jarell, Zad, and Bryan
